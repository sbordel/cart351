Watering the plants is an exploration of the aesthetic, semantic and collaborative value of digital gardens. Stemming visual inspiration from practices such as grid or vertical gardening, this virtual garden takes shape from a collage of samples randomly selected from images imported by the users.

⇾

As an organic garden would, this artificial interpretation reacts to changes in weather (according to the season) and needs human activity to remain healthy and grow. Users are invited to maintain the garden, either by contributing to its expansion by “planting” a new plot or by performing some general housekeeping (like watering or fertilizing).

⇾

This project was created by Samuelle Bordeleau in the context of CART351: Networks and Navigation @ Concordia University, a class taught by Sabine Rosenberg (who has offered a lot of her own time and knowledge in the creation of this website! Thank you, Sabine).

Watering the plants is still a work-in-progress and will continue to be a site for personal growth, practice, and experimentation in my web development adventures.
